# Future Directions

> [!summary]
> Research programmes that extend beyond the thesis's benchmark, drawn from the gaps in this vault.

> [!tip] In plain words
> The most promising next steps this line of research could take.

## Near-term (extends the thesis)
- **Beyond logistic ensembles:** sequence models over the predictor time-series; online/streaming detection.
- **Cost-aware predictors:** Pareto-optimise accuracy vs compute ([[Common Evaluation Metrics]]); cheap proxies for expensive signals.
- **More architectures/optimisers:** MLPs, deeper transformers, Muon vs AdamW timescale effects ([[12 - Modern Developments]]).
- **Anti-grokking causes:** *why* late collapse happens, not just detection ([[Anti-Grokking]]).

## Mid-term (new science)
- **Universal order parameter search:** test whether spectral ([[Heavy-Tailed Self-Regularization]]), geometric ([[Neural Collapse]], [[Neural Manifolds]]), or information ([[Information-Theoretic Measures]]) signals transfer best across tasks ([[Phase Transitions Across Models]]).
- **Theory of epoch-wise double descent:** derive the grokking time-curve from high-dimensional theory with **feature learning** ([[The Generalization Puzzle]], [[Advani - High-dimensional Dynamics of Generalization Error]]).
- **Geometry-as-predictor:** test manifold dimension/radius and NC metrics as grokking predictors on algorithmic tasks ([[Li - Representations and Generalization in Artificial and Brain Neural Networks]], [[Papyan - Prevalence of Neural Collapse]]).

## Long-term (frontier)
- **Scaling to LLMs:** does any predictor give early warning of emergent capabilities (a possible grokking-at-scale)?
- **Cross-substrate unification:** one transition theory spanning transformers, tensor networks ([[Pomarico - Transfer Entropy and O-Information to Detect Grokking]]), and diffusion ([[Biroli - Dynamical Regimes of Diffusion Models]]).
- **Control, not just prediction:** intervene during the plateau to *trigger* grokking earlier.
